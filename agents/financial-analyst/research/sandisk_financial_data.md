## 1. Company Overview

Sandisk Corporation was spun off from Western Digital in February 2025. The company is a pure-play NAND flash memory and data storage solutions provider, headquartered in Milpitas, CA. It employs ~11,000 people.

**Key facts:**
- Ticker: SNDK (NASDAQ)
- Spinoff date: February 2025 (from WDC)
- CEO: David V. Goeckeler (Chairman & CEO)
- CFO: Luis Felipe Visoso
- NAND market share: ~12% (5th globally, per Counterpoint Research Q1 2026)
- Manufacturing: Joint venture with Kioxia (BiCS FLASH); JV extended to 2034
- 10th-gen 3D NAND (300+ layers) entering production in 2026

## 7. NAND Flash / SSD Memory Market Outlook (2026-2028)

### TrendForce Industry Revenue Estimates (per Motley Fool / Market Digests, June 2026)

| 2028E | Not yet published | - | - |

### Key Market Dynamics

1. **AI Data Center Demand Surpasses Mobile**: For the first time, AI data center demand for NAND flash has surpassed mobile demand -- a structural shift not expected until 2027+. AI training clusters require petabytes of high-speed storage for dataset ingestion, checkpoint saving, and model weight retrieval.

2. **Supply Discipline by Competitors**: Samsung (cutting NAND starts from 4.9M to 4.68M wafers) and SK Hynix (10% NAND wafer cut) are redirecting fab capacity toward higher-margin HBM DRAM, constraining NAND supply and supporting pricing.

3. **Enterprise SSD Contract Prices**: Up >50% with further increases locked in for 2026.

4. **High Bandwidth Flash (HBF)**: Co-developed with SK Hynix; first samples targeted H2 2026; first AI inference devices expected early 2027. Could deliver 8-16x capacity per stack vs HBM.

5. **Agentic AI**: Growing adoption of agentic AI applications further increases demand for memory/compute, per TrendForce.

6. **NAND Market Share (Q1 2026)**: Samsung ~32%, SK Hynix ~19%, Kioxia ~15%, Sandisk ~12%, Micron ~11%.

### Risks to Outlook
- NAND is a commodity market; prices can fall 40-60% within 12 months (as in 2022-2023)
- Hyperscaler CapEx pause or macro recession could reverse pricing environment
- Current margins are at cycle peak levels
- Western Digital still holds a meaningful stake and continues to monetize (secondary offering of $3.09B in Feb 2026, debt-for-equity exchange in May 2026)
- Customer concentration risk (hyperscaler dependence)

## 8. Management Commentary & Strategic Direction

**CEO David Goeckeler (Q3 FY2026 Earnings):**
- "NAND demand is exceeding supply, and this trend is expected to continue well into next year."
- SanDisk has repositioned from consumer flash brand to AI infrastructure provider
- HBF (High Bandwidth Flash) with SK Hynix targeting OCP standardization
- 10th-gen BiCS FLASH (300+ layers) entering production in 2026

**Key Strategic Initiatives:**
1. Pure-play NAND focus (post-WDC split)
2. HBF co-development with SK Hynix for AI inference memory
3. Kioxia JV extended to 2034 for manufacturing
4. Enterprise/AI datacenter SSD mix shift (higher margins)
5. Reducing WDC ownership overhang through secondary offerings
