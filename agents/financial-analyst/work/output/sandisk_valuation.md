## 4. DCF Assumptions

### 4.1 Discount Rate: 12.0%

**Justification:** Per Lyn Alden's framework, publicly traded stocks are valued at 10-12%. We use the upper bound (12%) because:

1. **NAND Flash Cyclicality:** The memory industry experiences boom-bust pricing cycles with 30-40% revenue swings (demonstrated historically by Samsung Memory, Micron, SK Hynix). Current 70% operating margins are likely near peak cycle levels.
2. **Spin-off Execution Risk:** SanDisk is newly independent from WDC. Standalone overhead, IT system separation, and operational restructuring create normalized cost uncertainty.
3. **Competitive Intensity:** Samsung, Micron, Kioxia, and Chinese YMTC all compete aggressively in NAND flash. Oversupply can crash pricing rapidly.
4. **Technology Risk:** Transition to PLC (penta-level cell), 3D NAND scaling limits, and emerging storage-class memory technologies.

We use the midpoint between TTM FCF ($4.46B) and Q1 2026 annualized FCF ($12.0B). This avoids over-reliance on either:

## 8. What the Market Is Pricing In

The stock trades at a forward P/E of 9.4x, which implies the market expects approximately $32B in forward net income. To justify the current $1,916/share price, the market is pricing in:

1. **Starting FCF of $14-16B** (above even the Q1'26 annualized $12B run-rate)
2. **25-30% FCF growth** for 3-4 years (acceleration from current levels)
3. **Year 10 FCF of $50-70B**
4. **Terminal enterprise value of $600-800B**

These assumptions would require:
- AI data center demand to accelerate beyond current levels
- NAND flash pricing to remain at historic peaks indefinitely
- No meaningful competitive response from Samsung/Micron/Kioxia
- No industry cycle downturn

This is a demanding set of assumptions for a cyclical hardware business.

## 10. Key Risks

1. **NAND Flash Cyclicality:** The memory industry is notoriously cyclical. Current peak-cycle metrics (70% operating margins, 34% FCF margins) likely overstate normalized earnings potential. Historical cycles show 30-40% revenue swings.

2. **Competitive Landscape:** Samsung, Micron, Kioxia, and Chinese YMTC compete aggressively in NAND flash. Oversupply can crash prices rapidly, and YMTC's capacity additions are an overhang on industry pricing.

3. **AI Demand Sustainability:** Current growth is heavily AI-driven. If AI/ML capex spending normalizes or disappoints, enterprise SSD demand could slow sharply.

4. **Spin-off Execution Risk:** SanDisk is newly independent from WDC. Operational separation, IT systems, and standalone overhead costs create uncertainty in the normalized cost structure.

5. **Customer Concentration:** Large cloud hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP) represent significant portions of enterprise SSD demand.

6. **Technology Transitions:** 3D NAND scaling is approaching physical limits. Transition costs for new architectures (PLC, 3D DRAM alternatives) create technology risk.

7. **Spin-off Capital Structure:** The current low debt ($182M) may be a temporary post-spin-off feature. Future debt issuance for capacity investments could change the risk profile.

## 11. Limitations & Caveats

- **Short Trading History:** SNDK has only been trading independently since early 2025. Historical financial data (2023-2024) is carved out from WDC and may not accurately reflect stand-alone costs.
- **Extraordinary Growth Phase:** The recent revenue ramp from $1.7B to $5.95B quarterly is unprecedented for a mature tech hardware company. Predicting the trajectory of a business in hypergrowth is inherently uncertain.
- **Forward P/E Mismatch:** The Forward P/E of 9.4x vs. Trailing P/E of 63.7x is unusually wide, suggesting massive expected earnings growth. If this materializes, the stock could be fairly valued. However, NAND flash industry history suggests peak earnings rarely sustain.
- **Comparable Difficulty:** No pure-play NAND flash company trades independently at this scale. Micron and Samsung are diversified (DRAM + NAND + other). This limits the usefulness of peer multiples.

## 12. Investment Conclusion

### SELL / UNDERPERFORM

**Verdict:** SanDisk's business fundamentals are strong, with AI-driven demand creating a powerful growth trajectory. However, the current market price of $1,916 has fully priced in — and exceeded — even optimistic DCF scenarios. The stock embeds assumptions for sustained peak-cycle margins, continued market share gains, and no industry downturn.

At $1,916/share, the market is pricing SanDisk as if it will sustain and grow from current peak FCF levels indefinitely. NAND flash history argues against this. Companies in this industry (Micron, Samsung Memory) have historically seen 30-50% drawdowns from cycle peaks.

**A reasonable entry point would be $800-1,100/share**, representing ~15-30% margin of safety relative to base case intrinsic value.
